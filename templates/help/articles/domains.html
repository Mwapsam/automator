A sending domain is the domain your email comes from — for example mail.yourcompany.com. You manage all of yours on the Domains page.

Add a domain

  1. Open Domains.
  2. Under Add a sending domain, type your domain and click Provision.
  3. The new domain appears as a card with its DNS records ready to copy.
Tip: many businesses use a subdomain like mail. or send. for sending. This keeps your main domain's email (e.g. your inbox at Google Workspace) untouched.

What the statuses mean

StatusMeaning
Pending DNSThe domain is added but we haven't found your DNS records yet. Add the records, then click Check DNS.
VerifiedYour DNS is confirmed and the domain is ready to send and host mailboxes.
FailedProvisioning hit an error on the mail server. Try again, or contact support if it persists.
DisabledYou've paused the domain — it stays configured but won't send.

Check & verify a domain

After adding your DNS records, click Check DNS on the domain card. Each record is marked Found or Not found yet, and the domain verifies as soon as your ownership record is live. The card re-checks on its own every few seconds, so you can simply leave it open. If records aren't showing up, see the DNS setup guide and the troubleshooting guide.

Enable or disable a domain

Use Disable to temporarily stop a domain from sending without deleting it; click Enable to turn it back on. The configuration and records are preserved either way.

Delete a domain

Deleting a domain is permanent and also removes all of its mailboxes and aliases. You'll be asked to confirm. If you only want to pause sending, disable it instead.
