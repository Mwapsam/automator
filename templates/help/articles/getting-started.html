Welcome! This guide walks you through everything needed to start sending email from your own domain. The whole setup usually takes 15–30 minutes, most of which is waiting for DNS changes to take effect.

The big picture

To send email through the platform you need three things, in order:

  1. A sending domain (for example mail.yourcompany.com) added to your workspace.
  2. A few DNS records added at your domain provider so the internet trusts your mail.
  3. A verified domain — once your DNS records are live, we confirm them and switch the domain on.

After that you can create mailboxes, set up aliases, and (on supported plans) send through the API.

Step 1 — Add your domain

Go to Domains and enter the domain you want to send from, then click Provision. We'll generate your DNS records automatically.

See Add & configure a sending domain for details.

Step 2 — Add your DNS records

Copy the DKIM, SPF, DMARC and verification records shown on the domain card into your DNS provider's dashboard. Our DNS setup guide has step-by-step instructions for Cloudflare, GoDaddy, Namecheap and others.

Step 3 — Check DNS

Once the records are saved, click Check DNS on the domain card. Each record shows Found or Not found yet, so you can see exactly what's live. DNS can take a few minutes to a few hours to propagate — the page keeps re-checking automatically, so a domain usually verifies itself once your records go live.

Step 4 — Create mailboxes (optional)

With a verified domain you can create real mailboxes and forwarding aliases on the Mailboxes page. See Create mailboxes & aliases.

Step 5 — Invite your team

Bring colleagues into your workspace from Settings → Team. See Invite & manage your team.

Your progress is tracked on your dashboard and the onboarding checklist — you can always pick up where you left off.
